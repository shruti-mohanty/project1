A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile man of India

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (15 October 1931 â€“ 27 July 2015) was an Indian aerospace scientist and statesman who served as the 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born and raised in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and studied physics and aerospace engineering. He spent the next four decades as a scientist and science administrator, mainly at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and was intimately involved in India's civilian space programme and military missile development efforts.[1] He thus came to be known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.[2][3][4] He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by India in 1974.[5] Kalam was elected as the 11th president of India in 2002 with the support of both the ruling Bharatiya Janata Party and the then-opposition Indian National Congress. Widely referred to as the "People's President",[6] he returned to his civilian life of education, writing and public service after a single term. He was a recipient of several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour. While delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, Kalam collapsed and died from an apparent cardiac arrest on 27 July 2015, aged 83.[7] Thousands, including national-level dignitaries, attended the funeral ceremony held in his hometown of Rameswaram, where he was buried with full state honours.[8

Biographies

  • Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • Came from a humble background and faced financial challenges during his childhood.
  • Graduated in aeronautical engineering from Madras Institute of Technology in 1958.
  • Joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1963, contributing to India's space program.
  • Played a key role in the development of India's first satellite, Aryabhata.
  • Later, joined the Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and contributed to missile development, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles.
  • Became the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister of India in 1992.
  • Led the successful Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 as the Chief Project Coordinator.
  • Elected as the 11th President of India in 2002, serving until 2007.
  • During his presidency, he focused on youth development and initiated programs like the "Missile Man of India."
  • After his presidency, continued to inspire students and young people through lectures and interactions.
  • Passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
  • Known as the "People's President" and "Missile Man of India" for his contributions to science and education.
